Klara and the Sun

Summary

Klara, a solar-powered Artificial Friend, observes human life from a store window until she is

purchased to care for a sick girl named Josie. Her worldview is shaped by her unwavering faith

in the healing power of the Sun. As she serves Josie, Klara witnesses the complexity of human

relationships and the burdens placed on children in a society obsessed with genetic enhancement.

She offers loyalty and empathy, despite not being human herself. Klara’s belief in sacrifice leads

her to take a mysterious act of devotion to help Josie heal. Though ultimately replaced, Klara's

reflections highlight questions about love, consciousness, and purpose. The novel offers a

poignant, quiet look at humanity through artificial eyes.
