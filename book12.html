The Diary of a Young Girl

Summary

This is the real-life diary of Anne Frank, a Jewish girl who, along with her family, went into

hiding in a secret annex in Amsterdam to avoid Nazi persecution. Over the course of two years,

Anne writes about her life in hiding, her fears, her hopes, and her evolving thoughts on identity,

humanity, and the impact of war. Anne’s writing is candid, insightful, and poignant, offering a

glimpse into the daily struggles and emotional challenges of living under constant threat.

Ultimately, Anne’s diary is a testament to the resilience of the human spirit and a powerful

record of the Holocaust.
